Links:

My profiles:

  • Battle of the Bits -- music and art competition site; contains a lot of the music I've ever made, spanning over a decade. Recently lost the domain name, went from .org to .com as a result. There may be several links on the site that still use the .org address, sorry.
  • The Mod Archive -- more of the music I've made; a lot of the older songs are bad, but are still quite a few good ones.
  • TIC-80 -- games I've made for the TIC-80 fantasy computer; most notably contains eyesoul, a floaty, bouncy shoot-em-up game I wrote for a game jam.
  • Twitter -- mostly retweets, but a few old projects are shown here and occasionally I'll post something; a lot of my posts involve fighting games.
  • Soundcloud -- more music I've made, most of it like ten years old as of this writing. The songs here are pretty decent actually, and I recently posted a Toki Pona song I wrote with Renoise and Hatsune Miku V4X.
  • Sketchfab -- a handful of basic 3D models that I've made with Wings3D.
  • YouTube -- more music and some footage of a few old projects (really wish I knew where I had put the code for some of these, I kind of want to work on type.prime again); someone namejacked me back in 2006 so I'm null1023 there.

Other sites of interest:

  • Renoise -- a tracker style DAW, probably the best money I've ever spent regarding software.
  • MilkyTracker -- a music editor styled after the classic Fast Tracker II.
  • BambooTracker -- a tracker for making PC-98 music.
    • SUDDEN DESU -- a rather cool digital archaeology blog featuring deep technical analysis of hidden things in various games. Loads of info I've never heard of anywhere else.
  • Toki Pona -- the official Toki Pona website.
  • lipu sona -- a website for learning Toki Pona.
  • rnd's website -- personal website of the person who worked on the above Toki Pona course; largely technical content.
  • 12 days of sona pi toki pona -- a video course by jan Misali for learning Toki Pona.
  • seximal -- a website by jan Misali that promotes the seximal (base 6) numbering system. I'm not actually a fan, but I can at least understand the idea. :P
  • Wings3D -- a rather easy to use 3D modeling program that I like.
  • Toastytech -- Nathan's Toasty Technology page, famous for its Graphical User Interface Gallery.
  • Astronomy Picture of the Day -- NASA's long-running daily astronomy photo site.
  • Solipsys Limited -- a neat site with info on math and juggling among other things; the site is also an old-school, non-Wikipedia style wiki.
  • Darths and Droids -- a photo-webcomic that re-imagines the plot of the Star Wars movies as a tabletop RPG, starting from Episode 1, and expected to go through to Episode 9.
  • lipu tenpo -- a webzine written entirely in Toki Pona. If you're looking for a good collection of writings in Toki Pona, this is probably the best resource.
  • musi lili -- a site by jan Same featuring a lot of resources on Toki Pona. In particular, this site has a rather nice font known as linja pona that represents the sitelen pona script for Toki Pona.
  • Shmups Forum -- forum dedicated to talking about scrolling shoot-em-up games; possibly the only remaining phpbb-style forum that I still visit.
